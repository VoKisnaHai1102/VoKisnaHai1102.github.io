What to Expect

Wildlife Sightings: Kanha is home to the majestic Bengal tiger, but also look out for barasingha (swamp deer), gaur (Indian bison), sambar, chital, wild boar, jackal, and a variety of bird species.

Sal Forests: The park features beautiful sal forests, open grasslands, and bamboo thickets that create a diverse habitat for wildlife. The meadows or "maidans" are excellent spots for wildlife viewing.

Photography Tips: Bring a camera with a good zoom lens (at least 300mm) for wildlife photography. Early morning and late afternoon light create magical photos. Keep your camera ready at all times as wildlife sightings can happen unexpectedly.

Conservation Success: Kanha is known for its successful conservation efforts, particularly for saving the barasingha from extinction. From just 66 in the 1970s, the population has grown to over 800 today.

Weather Note: Mornings can be chilly even in warm months. Dress in layers and wear neutral colors (avoid bright colors that may disturb wildlife). Bring a hat, sunglasses, and sunscreen for sun protection.
