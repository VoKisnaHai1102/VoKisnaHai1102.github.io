What to Expect

Natural Wonder: Dhuandhar Falls derives its name from the Sanskrit words 'Dhuan' (smoke) and 'Dhar' (flow), as the powerful cascade creates a mist that looks like smoke rising from the waters.

Marble Rocks: The Bhedaghat area is famous for its marble cliffs that rise to 100 feet on either side of the Narmada River, stretching for about 3 km. The white marble takes on beautiful colors as the sun's rays reflect off them.

Photography Tips: Morning light is ideal for photographing the falls, while evening light creates magical reflections on the marble rocks. Protect your camera from water spray near the falls.

Local Crafts: Consider purchasing marble artifacts created by local artisans as souvenirs â€“ these include decorative items, jewelry boxes, and figurines crafted from the same marble that forms the cliffs.

Seasonal Note: The waterfall is most spectacular during and after the monsoon season (July-October). During winter and summer, the water flow may be regulated by upstream dams.
